Visualizations: 
Pair 1: Physical Characteristics by Position  (Sean) 
Table: A grouped summary table showing mean and SD of height_inches, wingspan_inches, and wingspan_advantage for each of the five positions (PG, SG, SF, PF, C).
Visualization: A side-by-side box plot with position on the x-axis and wingspan/height on the y-axis, faceted to show both measurements. 


Pair 2: Performance by Wingspan Advantage Tier (Charlie)
Table: Players binned into Low (<4"), Medium (4–6"), and High (>6") wingspan advantage tiers, with average blk, trb, stl, and pts for each group.


Visualization: A faceted bar chart with wingspan tier on the x-axis and separate panels for each stat (blk, trb, stl). If the hypothesis holds, you'd see a clear staircase pattern from Low to High across defensive stats.




Pair 3: Player-Level Wingspan Extremes and Performance (Suhas)
Table: The 10 players with the highest and 10 with the lowest wingspan_advantage, showing their position, height_inches, pts, blk, trb, and stl.
Visualization: A scatter plot with wingspan_advantage on the x-axis and blk or trb on the y-axis, points colored by position, with player names labeled at the extremes using ggrepel. Shows the continuous relationship and whether it holds across positions.
